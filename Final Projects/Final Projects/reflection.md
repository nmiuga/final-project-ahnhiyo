#  Reflection

## Overall Learnings
I learned that making a decider app is all about getting the user experience flow correctly. Making the front page a "surprise" is a more exciting way to cut down the fatigue that comes from decision-making and pushes people to make quick choices. However, I also realized how important it is to give users more control. So, I also added sections for refining filters, Tinder-like swiping, and favoriting locations to keep them engaged longer.

Working with the Google Places API taught me that a lot of places do not have complete details, like photos, hours, websites, and prices, so I had to enlist AI to help me build the app in a way that still looks visually appealing and usable even wehn the API is missing things. Codex helped a lot here, particularly with using outside sources to fill in the data gaps.

## Challenges
The hardest issue for me, though, was layout consistency. Getting cards to stay the same width/size and not shift around based on image ratios or text length took way longer than I expected. I learned that when you work with AI, you can’t be vague. Once I started giving clearer directions (exact heights, max widths, line limits), the results immediately improved and the UI finally started feeling complete.

## Next Steps
I want to fix the launch screen and expand filtering (e.g. dietary options, time-of-day). I would also want to have a main screen load before the app generates its initial surprise location.

## Course Experience
I had a great time working on the projects, and I honestly felt like I learned the most when things didn’t work on the first try. AI doesn’t give perfect results every time, but it was always exciting to see what it could create from a simple prompt and then shape it into something better through iteration. It was definitely challenging, but it was also really rewarding to keep tweaking and refining until the app started to feel real and usable.
